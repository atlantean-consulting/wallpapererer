## A Note from the Founder

The following will probably rile up both sides of this debate, which is kind of the point. If you cling to your worldview and are unwilling to consider alternative points of view, then go back to your echo chamber. But if you possess the courage of an open mind, read on.

Atlantean Consulting supports the use of large-language models (LLMs), which are commonly called "AI". When properly prompted and curated, we have found them to be incredibly useful; and we use them extensively in our work. We freely admit to this, because we are honest people. No doubt this will irritate AI-doomsayers who think that all code ought to be organic, free-range, and typed by human hands.

That being said, Atlantean Consulting vehemently opposes the use of LLMs or artificial intelligence in ways that serve the interests of capital, or which generally do not contribute to human flourishing, human freedom, and human happiness; *e.g.*: increasing the workload of human beings without a commensurate increase in total compensation; replacing human beings with LLMs or agentic systems without compensation; using AI for surveillance of human beings; or worst of all, using or deploying AI to end human lives.

There are those who claim that *any* use of LLMs/AI is, *ipso facto*, the replacement of human thought. To these people we point out that were it not for Claude Code, almost none of the software in our repository would exist at all. We (inclusive of us and Claude) wrote it because we felt it would be useful to people. We released it publicly, for free (-as-in-freedom and -as-in-beer) so that others could read it, use it, and improve on it, in the true spirit of the open-source movement. This is why we license our software under the GNU GPL.

Despite this, however, we object in the strongest terms to any ingestion of our code into ~~Microsoft's~~ Macro$lop's "GitHub Copilot", for use as training data. ([Link](https://www.theregister.com/2026/03/26/github_ai_training_policy_changes/), for your reference) We have "opted-out" of this program and encourage you to do so as well. We did this for several reasons:

* Macro$slop represents the antithesis of free software. It has been aggressively hostile to the FOSS movement for fifty years.
* Macro$lop (along with Amazon, Google, Apple, *etc.*) is the *sine qua non* of capital, which we oppose.
* Macro$lop founder Bill Gates was a close associate of Jeffrey Epstein. (This fact doesn't directly bear on the use of GitHub user data, but it is icky and shows what kind of people Macro$lop employs) And most importantly:
* *Copilot is objectively garbage*. This isn't just us being whiny Macro$lop haters. We've tried it. It consistently produces low-quality code that often does not work.

We are not so naive as to think that this notice (or, for that matter, our opting-out) will dissuade Macro$lop from using our code in their training data. We include it only as a matter of conscience and as a political statement.
